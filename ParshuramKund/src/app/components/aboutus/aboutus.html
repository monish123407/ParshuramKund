About Parshuram Kund

The Holy Pilgrimage & The Kumbh of Northeast

Welcome to Parshuram Kund

Parshuram Kund is a revered Hindu pilgrimage site located in the lower reaches of the Lohit River, on the Brahmaputra plateau in the Lohit district of Arunachal Pradesh, India. Known famously as the "Kumbh of the Northeast", this holy site draws thousands of devotees and tourists every year.

Nestled amidst the majestic hills of Arunachal Pradesh, the Kund is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, offering spiritual solace and a glimpse into India's rich cultural and mythological heritage.

Mythological Significance

The history of Parshuram Kund is deeply rooted in ancient Indian scriptures, finding mention in the Srimad Bhagavat, Kalika Purana, and the epic Mahabharata.

According to legend, the great sage Parshuram (the sixth avatar of Lord Vishnu) committed the grave sin of matricide at the behest of his father, the temperamental sage Jamadagni. To atone for this sin, he was directed to undertake a pilgrimage to the Brahmakund.

It is believed that when Parshuram took a dip in the holy waters of this Kund, his sin was completely washed away, and the axe—which had become fused to his hand due to his deed—finally fell off. The spot where his axe dropped is known as Parshuram Kund.

The Holy Dip & Makar Sankranti

Pilgrims from all over India and neighboring countries visit the Kund to take a holy bath in the waters of the Lohit River, especially during the auspicious occasion of Makar Sankranti (January 14th).

Bathing in the Kund during this period is considered to be of immense spiritual significance. Devotees believe that the holy waters cleanse them of all sins and lead to spiritual liberation (Moksha).

The District Administration of Lohit, in coordination with various departments, works tirelessly to facilitate a safe, clean, and organized experience for the pilgrims, providing lodging, drinking water, medical aid, and security.

The Parshuram Kund Mela

The annual Parshuram Kund Mela is a grand celebration that showcases the vibrant local culture and tradition. It is a meeting point for diverse ethnic communities of Arunachal Pradesh and pilgrims from all states of India.

During the Mela, a local Craft Centre and exhibitions are set up where visitors can purchase traditional handloom, handicrafts, and artifacts. The festival promotes national integration, cultural exchange, and eco-tourism in the region.

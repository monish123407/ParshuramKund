About Parshuram Kund

Parshuram Kund is a revered Hindu pilgrimage site located in the lower reaches of the Lohit River, on the Brahmaputra plateau in the Lohit district of Arunachal Pradesh, India. Famously referred to as the "Kumbh of the Northeast", this holy sanctuary draws lakhs of devotees and tourists from across the globe every January.

Mythological Significance

The history of Parshuram Kund is deeply rooted in ancient scriptures, finding mentions in the Srimad Bhagavat, Kalika Purana, and the epic Mahabharata.

According to legend, the great sage Lord Parshuram (the sixth incarnation of Lord Vishnu) committed the grave sin of matricide at his father's behest. To atone for this deed, he took a dip in the holy waters of this Kund. His sin was washed away, and the axe—which was fused to his hand due to the crime—finally fell off.

Holy Dip & Makar Sankranti

Pilgrims gather at the Kund for the sacred bath in the Lohit River, particularly during the auspicious festival of Makar Sankranti in mid-January.

Devotees believe that a holy dip in the crystal-clear waters during this transition cleanses all earthly sins, bringing peace and leading to spiritual liberation (Moksha). The District Administration of Lohit ensures comfortable lodging, clean drinking water, security, and emergency services for a seamless pilgrimage.

Experience the Spiritual Harmony

The annual Parshuram Kund Mela is a grand confluence of spiritual faith and diverse cultural elements. It serves as a meeting point for tribal communities of Arunachal Pradesh and pilgrims from all corners of India, fostering unity and eco-tourism.
